About Anti-Back-Ward

Anti-Back-Ward is a 2d puzzle platformer made by myself, with assets created by my friends, for the 2023 GMTK (Game Makers Tool Kit) Game Jam. You play as a white cat in a surrealist looping world, trying to get to a floating mirror and progress to the next level.

Gameplay

Anti-Back-Ward's core gameplay boils down to navigating to the levels objective (a small floating mirror), and collecting the optional coins along the way. In order to traverse the levels you have to utilize basic 2d platforming, the purple mirror dimension tiles, and navigate around the green blockers.

Throughout the levels you can find pink / magenta platforms. When standing on these platforms and activating your special ability, they start looping (in a mirrored fashion) as well. These are called the mirror portals, and they make up the majority of the puzzle aspect. While they are active, you can only interact with them, and a few other objects. If your move is used again, the mirror portal closes, potentially killing you or letting you interact with a new portal in a new place.

Theming

The theming of the game leans towards abstract. The music (Made by Jifyrex) is a slow, surreal track, while the art (Made by ) is predominantly mute tones, with select highlights. These highlights are reserved for mirror platforms (purple and magenta), the end goal and pickups (gold and cyan), and the blocker enemies (dark green). The background consists of abstract gray, light gray and near white patterns using a small selection of tiles. Theses patterns hint at how the level can be pieced together.

Level Design

  • Level 1

    The goal of a first level is to introduce concepts quickly and concisely. The layout of this level is setup to teach the player the scale of the players movement, and the basics of gameplay.

  • Level 2

    Like level 1, this level immediately shows the player the objective, but it is out of reach. Because this level is introducing the looping mechanic for the first time, it's kept to one axis. The staircase acts as a one way barrier, further demonstrating the looping nature of the game (especially to anyone who misses the jump)

  • Level 3

    This is the first level with a) the mirror region and b) any hazard. The objective is across a pit of spikes that are far too wide to jump. The only option the player has is to follow the prompt and press enter while standing on the region. While the geometry might seem a bit random, it is actually setup to force the player to stand directly on the region. Then, it shows that the player can now walk through walls (with the help of a coin indicator) and that the player is likely to die in a wall if the exit the mirror mode in the wrong spot.

  • Level 4

    This is the first 'real' level, though it keeps the concepts minimal. It starts with a small platforming section, before teaching the player about how spikes and walls are ignored while in the mirror region, through the use of a staircase that can't normally be traversed.

  • Level 5

    Level 5 stands out as a very simplistic puzzle. The object is trapped in the ground below you, but there is a mirror region with a divot that lets you access it. Unfortunately, that divot does not line up. At least at first. By continuously wrapping the screen, due to the co-prime sizes of the region and the room, it eventually lines up.

  • Level 6

    Level 6 introduces the last new mechanic, and is the first level with more than 1 mirror region. The new blocker enemy provides both an obstacle to be cleared with the mirror region and a helping platform as the player descends to the goal. Uniquely, the blocker can be interacted with while in the mirror mode, but can also be crushed by the mirror mode, as this level demonstrates.

  • Level 7

    Level 7 requires an understanding of all the mechanics, and forces the player to unravel the intended solution. The puzzle is solved by using the mirror dimension and screen wrapping to reach platforms you otherwise couldn't, in order to clear the area with the end goal.

  • Level 8

    Level 8 acts as a send off to the mirror mechanic. It features the most mirror sections, but each one only opens up access to a few nearby locations. The challenge of this level is not from piecing together mechanics, but instead by understanding them well enough to not get caught out.
